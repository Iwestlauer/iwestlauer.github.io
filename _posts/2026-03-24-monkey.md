If I had to describe myself in one image, it might just be a curious little monkey—wide-eyed, always reaching for something new, and maybe getting into a bit of mischief along the way. I’ve always been inquisitive by nature, the kind of person who wants to understand how things work, take them apart, and put them back together (hopefully better than before). There’s something playful in that process too—learning, experimenting, sometimes failing, but always moving forward.

Lately, that curiosity has found a new playground: machine learning. This semester, I’ve been diving into classification models and Retrieval-Augmented Generation (RAG), and it honestly feels like climbing through the branches of a really fascinating (and occasionally confusing) jungle. Each concept unlocks another layer—how models learn patterns, how they make decisions, and how we can enhance them with external knowledge to make them even more powerful.

There’s something incredibly exciting about seeing these ideas click. One moment you’re staring at equations and code that feel completely foreign, and the next, you start to recognize patterns and connections. It’s that spark—that “aha” moment—that keeps me coming back, just like a monkey reaching for the next branch.

Right now, I’m gearing up for my final exam, which feels a bit like preparing for a big leap. There’s a mix of excitement and nerves, but mostly I’m proud of how much I’ve learned in such a short time. No matter what happens, I know I’ve grown—and that I’m not done exploring yet.

So here I am: curious, playful, and always learning. Maybe a little like that monkey after all.
